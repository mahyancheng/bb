Trivia

Gozan (
五山
) refers to the Five Mountains and Ten Monasteries System (
五山十刹制度
,
Gozan Jissetsu Seido
), which describes Zen Buddhist temples and monasteries built on isolated mountains. Though the practice originated in India, the Five Mountains system was adapted by Japan with two sets of five built in Kyoto and Kamakura during the 12th century.
