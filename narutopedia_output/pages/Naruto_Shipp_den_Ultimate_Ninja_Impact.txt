Naruto Shippūden: Ultimate Ninja Impact
 is the sixth and final
Naruto
 instalment of the
PlayStation Portable
. It was released in North America on 19 October 2011, in Japan on 20 October 2011 and on 21 October 2011 in Europe.

Gameplay

The game starts from
Kazekage Rescue Mission
 and goes all the way up to the
Five Kage Summit
. The gameplay itself is very similar to
Dynasty Warriors
. Cards can be used to increase characters' stats, provide special effects during battle, change certain characters' movesets, Secret Techniques & Awakenings and clear obstacles on Ultimate Road map. Every playable character can equip upto 4 cards before starting a mission. When equiping certain cards together, a Team effect can be activated, further increasing stats and providing more effect in battle.

Features

Re-Live Current Naruto Storylines – Fans can experience their favourite anime storylines with crisp animation cut scenes in full VO and fight in epic battle sequences while executing famous finishing moves like Wind Style: Rasenshuriken that re-creates the anime storyline

Large Scale Battles – Test your skills against hordes of attacking enemy ninja in frenzied large scale melee battles & emerge victorious using famous moves like Naruto's Big Ball Rasengan & Shikamaru's Shadow Sewing Technique!

360° Rotating Camera – Upgraded camera views enhance battles sequences that offer an exciting and immersive gameplay experience

New Anime Characters – Over 50 characters including gigantics like Eight-Tails, Kakuzu & Gamabunta, including Awakenings features that enable incredible power and defence for short bursts

Deep Missions – Over 100+ unique battles to experience over 8 stages and 25 custom designed missions

Card Collection – Collect over 300+ character cards, create card combinations to enable special character power-ups

Sole Training- Kill at least 1-1000 hordes of enemies but only one player.

Ad-Hoc Connected Play – Battle enemy ninja hordes together with friends in Tag Battle mode

Trivia

The game features soundtracks from
Naruto Shippūden: Ultimate Ninja Heroes 3
 and
Naruto Shippūden: Legends: Akatsuki Rising
.

It is the last Naruto game to be released on the PSP.

This was the first Naruto game to have a CERO B rating in Japan. All previous Naruto games had an A rating.
