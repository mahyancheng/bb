Kiri
 (
キリ
,
Kiri
) is a
medical-nin
 from
Kirigakure
.

Personality

In the anime, Kiri harbours hostility towards the shinobi of
Iwagakure
 for having killed his father.
 He is a focused shinobi who cares about his comrades' lives.

Appearance

Kiri has short spiky, black hair and black eyes. He wears the standard attire of a Kirigakure shinobi inclusive of
flak jacket
, and pin-stripped leg and arm-guards. He also wears the
forehead protector
 of the
Allied Shinobi Forces
.

Abilities

As a
medical-nin
, Kiri is able to perform the
Mystical Palm Technique
 to heal one's injuries.
 He was also shown to be a keen strategist, aiding Kankurō in the planning of an ambush.

Part II

Paradise Life on a Boat

Main article:
Paradise Life on a Boat

In the anime, Kiri was amongst a group of Kiri-nin that followed
Ganryū
 to a small island off the coast of the
Land of Water
 with the intention of killing the messenger from
Iwagakure
. When Ganryū later told them to fall back, after hearing that their actions were violating the will of the
Fifth Mizukage
, Kiri was amongst the shinobi that voiced their discontent with this. Nevertheless they retreated after getting attacked by
Naruto
's shadow clones.

Fourth Shinobi World War: Confrontation






Kiri healing Tokuma.



During the
Fourth Shinobi World War
, Kiri was assigned to the
Surprise Attack Division
. After the division mobilised and found a good location to set up camp, Kankurō and Kiri begin to formulate battle strategies. Later, when
Zaji
 was injured, Kiri was asked to attend to him. Protected by
Ittan
's
rock shelter
, Kiri healed the injured, and after the explosion asked Zaji what was happening on the outside, and was reassured that everyone was still alive.

Later he and the part of his division encountered a back up for the
Surprise Attack and Diversion Platoon
. When they tried to flee from it, they were hit with
Ibuse
's
Poison Mist
 and succumbed to its effects. They are however rescued by the samurai of the
Fifth Division
 who carry them to the medical unit for treatment.
